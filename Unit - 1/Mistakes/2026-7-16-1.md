# 英语表达纠错笔记

## 1. Just work.

### 原句

Just work.

### 问题

这句话更像一个命令：

> Just work.
> 只管工作。/ 好好工作。

但你想表达的是“我今天只是工作了”。

### 更自然的表达

> I just worked today.

也可以说：

> I only worked today.
> I didn’t do much besides work today.

### 重点

描述已经发生的事情，需要用过去时：

* work → worked
* export → exported
* encounter → encountered

---

## 2. I am a Technical Artist.

### 原句

I am a Technical Artist.

### 更自然的表达

> I’m a technical artist.

### 问题

职业名称通常不需要大写，除非它是正式职位名称的一部分。

常见写法：

* a technical artist
* a software engineer
* a game designer
* an environment artist

### 重点

普通职业名称一般小写。

---

## 3. Today I exported gltf files form Unreal Engine to unity.

### 原句

Today I exported gltf files form Unreal Engine to unity.

### 修改

> Today, I exported some glTF files from Unreal Engine to Unity.

### 错误一：form → from

* form：形式、表格、形成
* from：从……

这里需要表示“从 Unreal Engine”，所以用：

> from Unreal Engine

### 错误二：unity → Unity

Unity 是产品名称，需要大写。

类似的专有名称：

* Unity
* Unreal Engine
* Blender
* Maya
* Houdini

### 错误三：gltf → glTF

更规范的写法是：

> glTF

### 错误四：缺少 some

直接说：

> I exported glTF files.

语法没有问题，但在日常表达中，加上 some 往往更自然：

> I exported some glTF files.

### 错误五：Today 后面可以加逗号

> Today, I exported some glTF files.

句首时间状语后加逗号，会更清晰。

---

## 4. A project needs that.

### 原句

A project needs that.

### 问题

这句话能被理解，但不太自然。

主要有两个原因：

第一，**a project** 太模糊。
第二，**that** 指代不够自然。

### 更自然的表达

> One of our projects required it.

或者：

> I needed to do that for a project.
> It was required for one of our projects.
> A project required those files.

### needs 和 required 的区别

你描述的是今天已经完成的工作，因此过去时更一致：

> required

而不是：

> needs

### it 和 that

当指代前面提到的任务或操作时，通常用 **it** 更自然：

> The project required it.

### 重点

讲已经完成的工作时，前后时态尽量保持一致。

---

## 5. I spent most of the day exporting glTF files.

### 句型

> spend + 时间 + doing something

意思是：

> 花费时间做某事

### 例句

> I spent two hours fixing bugs.
> 我花了两个小时修复错误。

> She spent the morning writing reports.
> 她花了一个上午写报告。

> We spent all day testing the build.
> 我们花了一整天测试版本。

### 为什么用 exporting？

因为在 **spend time** 后面，通常使用动词的 **-ing** 形式：

> spent the day exporting

不能说：

> I spent most of the day to export glTF files. ❌

### 重点

固定搭配：

> spend time doing something

---

## 6. Yes, the process is very smooth.

### 原句

Yes, the process is very smooth.

### 更自然的表达

> Yes, the process went very smoothly.

### 问题一：is → went

你描述的是刚才已经完成的过程，因此用过去时：

> went

### 问题二：smooth → smoothly

在这里，smoothly 修饰动词 went，所以需要使用副词。

* smooth：形容词
* smoothly：副词

对比：

> The process was smooth.
> 这个过程很顺利。

> The process went smoothly.
> 这个过程进行得很顺利。

### 两种正确结构

> The process was very smooth.

> The process went very smoothly.

### 重点

形容词修饰名词，副词修饰动词。

---

## 7. I encountered some problems.

### 原句

I encountered some problems.

### 评价

这句话语法正确，也很自然。

### 另一种更口语化的表达

> I ran into a few problems.

对比：

* encounter problems：稍正式
* run into problems：更口语化

### some 和 a few

两者都可以：

> some problems
> a few problems

**a few problems** 通常给人一种“有几个问题，但数量不多”的感觉。

---

## 8. but you GPT Sol help me resolved that.

### 原句

but you GPT Sol help me resolved that.

这句话有几个问题。

### 修改

> But GPT helped me solve them.

也可以说：

> But you helped me solve them.

---

### 错误一：help → helped

事情已经发生，因此使用过去时：

> helped

---

### 错误二：helped me resolved → helped me solve

在 **help + 人 + 动词** 结构中，后面的动词使用原形：

> help me solve
> helped me fix
> helped me understand

不能说：

> helped me solved ❌
> helped me resolved ❌

正确：

> helped me solve
> helped me resolve

### 句型

> help + someone + do something

例如：

> You helped me understand the problem.
> You helped me fix the bug.

---

### 错误三：that → them

前面说的是：

> some problems

problems 是复数，所以后面用：

> them

不能用单数的：

> that

对比：

> I encountered a problem, and GPT helped me solve it.

> I encountered some problems, and GPT helped me solve them.

---

### 错误四：you GPT

不能直接把 **you** 和 **GPT** 放在一起。

可以选择其中一种：

> You helped me.

或者：

> GPT helped me.

---

### 关于 “GPT Sol”

如果你指的是某个具体的 GPT 名称，需要写出准确名称；如果只是指 ChatGPT，直接说：

> GPT helped me.
> ChatGPT helped me.

---

## 9. And thank you very much.

### 原句

And thank you very much.

### 评价

这句话可以理解，也没有严重语法问题。

不过单独表达感谢时，通常直接说：

> Thank you very much.

更口语一些：

> Thanks a lot.
> Thank you so much.

如果与上一句连接，可以说：

> GPT helped me solve them, so thank you very much.

---

## 10. What different between resolve and solve?

### 原句

What different between resolve and solve?

### 正确表达

> What’s the difference between “resolve” and “solve”?

完整形式：

> What is the difference between “resolve” and “solve”?

### 错误一：different → difference

* different：形容词，不同的
* difference：名词，区别

这里需要名词：

> the difference

对比：

> They are different.
> 它们是不同的。

> What is the difference?
> 有什么区别？

### 错误二：缺少 is

疑问句需要：

> What is...?

或者缩写：

> What’s...?

### 固定句型

> What’s the difference between A and B?

例如：

> What’s the difference between “look” and “see”?

> What’s the difference between “job” and “work”?

---

# solve 和 resolve 的区别

## solve

重点是：

> 找到答案或解决办法

常见搭配：

* solve a problem
* solve a puzzle
* solve an equation
* solve a technical issue
* solve a mystery

例句：

> We solved the problem.

表示我们找到了问题的解决办法。

---

## resolve

重点是：

> 让问题、冲突或情况结束

常见搭配：

* resolve an issue
* resolve a conflict
* resolve a dispute
* resolve a complaint
* resolve a disagreement

例句：

> The team resolved the conflict.

表示团队结束了这场冲突。

---

## 两者都可以使用的情况

> solve an issue
> resolve an issue

两者都正确，但侧重点不同：

* solve：找出解决办法
* resolve：把事情彻底处理完

在你的语境中，更自然的是：

> GPT helped me solve the problems.

因为你强调的是 GPT 帮你找到了技术问题的解决方法。

# 你的整段话可以改成

> I just worked today. I’m a technical artist. I spent most of the day exporting some glTF files from Unreal Engine to Unity for a project. The process went very smoothly. I encountered a few problems, but GPT helped me solve them. Thank you very much.

更口语、更自然的版本：

> I mostly worked today. I’m a technical artist, and I spent most of the day exporting glTF files from Unreal Engine to Unity for a project. The process went pretty smoothly. I ran into a few problems, but GPT helped me solve them. Thanks a lot.

# 本次重点句型

1. **I spent most of the day doing something.**
   我花了大部分时间做某事。

2. **The process went smoothly.**
   过程进行得很顺利。

3. **I encountered / ran into a few problems.**
   我遇到了一些问题。

4. **Someone helped me solve the problem.**
   某人帮助我解决了问题。

5. **What’s the difference between A and B?**
   A 和 B 有什么区别？
